Tomorrows Playground

Overview Exhibition Objects

"Drifting Through Stillness"

A child rides his bike on a wet, foggy street surrounded by heavy traffic and looming skyscrapers. The scene juxtaposes the boy's simple, carefree action with the grim urban setting, marked by pollution and overdevelopment. The blurred headlights and the grey palette evoke a sense of suffocation, hinting at a world dominated by environmental neglect. The artist critiques humanity’s indifference to such conditions while capturing a moment of innocence persisting amidst decay. The boy’s obliviousness to his environment mirrors society’s denial of its ecological impact.

info@gmail.com
Through March 27, 2025
Isabel, Ha An, Carla, Philippa

"Dreams on Dried Soil"

Two children sit joyfully on a desolate, cracked earth, playing with buckets and molds as though they are on a sandy beach. The barren landscape contrasts sharply with their happiness, emphasizing resilience and the human ability to adapt to harsh realities. The artist underscores the stark normalcy of ecological collapse in this speculative future, where drought-stricken land becomes just another playground. The image critiques humanity’s inaction toward climate change while illustrating how children’s joy can exist even in the most tragic contexts.

info@gmail.com
Through March 27, 2025
Isabel, Ha An, Carla, Philippa

"Signals in the Silence"

A young girl stands in her backyard, holding a smartphone with a calm yet intent gaze, while her surroundings are damp and littered with debris. The juxtaposition of modern technology with a degraded environment highlights the ironic prioritization of digital connectivity over ecological preservation. The artist captures the girl’s innocence and quiet confidence, offering a haunting commentary on future generations’ normalization of environmental disaster. It’s a portrait of resilience but also a critique of misplaced values in a world teetering on the brink.

info@gmail.com
Through March 27, 2025
Isabel, Ha An, Carla, Philippa

"Crowns of Rust"

Two children stand in an abandoned amusement park, one wearing a "crown" crafted from discarded cans, while a derelict carousel looms in the background. The eerie setting juxtaposes their playful innocence with the desolation of consumerism’s aftermath. The artist critiques society’s overconsumption and disregard for sustainability, presenting a future where children reclaim the remnants of a broken world. The crown’s humor and irony underscore humanity’s wastefulness, while the children’s unconcerned demeanor reflects a generation born into decay yet thriving in their own way.

info@gmail.com
Through March 27, 2025
Isabel, Ha An, Carla, Philippa

"Lines in the Mud"

Two children sit at the edge of a polluted pond, holding up a goldfish amidst murky, debris-laden water. The golden fish, a symbol of hope and purity, contrasts with the dark, contaminated setting. Their casual joy implies a normalization of the unnatural, challenging the viewer to question their own desensitization to environmental degradation. The artist captures the dichotomy of beauty and decay, hinting at the coexistence of life and destruction. It’s a quiet but powerful critique of humanity's indifference to its surroundings.

Through March 27, 2025
Isabel, Ha An, Carla, Philippa

"The Celebration of Now"

A group of children, adorned with bright party hats, laugh as they gather around a birthday cake on a desolate, trash-strewn beach. Their joy contrasts starkly with the bleak surroundings, where the remnants of waste creep into the edges of the scene. The image captures the innocence of celebration amidst a backdrop of environmental neglect. The artist critiques humanity’s ability to ignore destruction while celebrating fleeting joys. It evokes a bittersweet realization of resilience in a fractured world.

"The Quiet Drift"

A lone child sits in a bright orange float, surrounded by stagnant water in the ruins of an abandoned building. The vibrant color of the float contrasts with the dark, overgrown and decaying environment, symbolizing life persisting in a forgotten space. The scene is eerily serene, hinting at a future where adaptation is the norm in desolate surroundings. The artist invites reflection on the persistence of innocence against a backdrop of abandonment. The quiet isolation amplifies the haunting beauty of resilience.
